Gamma and Beta Functions

Engineering Mathematics Project

Aim

To study Gamma and Beta functions, understand their mathematical properties, evaluate integrals using these functions, and explore their applications in engineering and science.

Introduction

Gamma and Beta functions are important special functions in mathematics. They are widely used in calculus, probability theory, physics, statistics, and engineering applications.

The Gamma function extends the concept of factorial to real and complex numbers, while the Beta function is closely related to the Gamma function.

Gamma Function

The Gamma function is defined as:

$$\Gamma(n)=\int_0^\infty x^{n-1}e^{-x}dx$$

All Formulae of Gamma and Beta Functions

1. Gamma Function Definition

$$\Gamma(n)=\int_{0}^{\infty} x^{n-1}e^{-x}dx$$

2. Relation with Factorial

$$\Gamma(n+1)=n!$$

3. Recursive Property

$$\Gamma(n+1)=n\Gamma(n)$$

4. Gamma of One

$$\Gamma(1)=1$$

5. Gamma of Half

$$\Gamma\left(\frac{1}{2}\right)=\sqrt{\pi}$$

6. Reflection Formula

$$\Gamma(n)\Gamma(1-n)=\frac{\pi}{\sin(\pi n)}$$

7. Duplication Formula

$$\Gamma(n)\Gamma\left(n+\frac{1}{2}\right)= \frac{\sqrt{\pi}}{2^{2n-1}}\Gamma(2n)$$

8. Another Integral Form

$$\Gamma(n)=2\int_{0}^{\infty}e^{-x^2}x^{2n-1}dx$$

9. Beta Function Definition

$$B(m,n)=\int_{0}^{1}x^{m-1}(1-x)^{n-1}dx$$

10. Relation Between Beta and Gamma Functions

$$B(m,n)=\frac{\Gamma(m)\Gamma(n)}{\Gamma(m+n)}$$

11. Symmetry Property

$$B(m,n)=B(n,m)$$

12. Trigonometric Form

$$B(m,n)=2\int_{0}^{\frac{\pi}{2}} \sin^{2m-1}\theta\cos^{2n-1}\theta\,d\theta$$

13. Sine-Cosine Integral Formula

$$\int_{0}^{\frac{\pi}{2}} \sin^{m}x\cos^{n}x\,dx= \frac{1}{2} B\left( \frac{m+1}{2}, \frac{n+1}{2} \right)$$

14. Recursive Formula

$$B(m,n+1)=\frac{n}{m+n}B(m,n)$$

15. Another Recursive Formula

$$B(m+1,n)=\frac{m}{m+n}B(m,n)$$

16. Important Special Values

$$B(1,1)=1$$
$$\Gamma(2)=1!$$
$$\Gamma(3)=2!$$
$$\Gamma(4)=3!=6$$

Relation with factorial:

$$\Gamma(n+1)=n!$$

Properties

  • \(\Gamma(1)=1\)
  • \(\Gamma(n+1)=n\Gamma(n)\)
  • \(\Gamma\left(\frac{1}{2}\right)=\sqrt{\pi}\)

Beta Function

The Beta function is defined as:

$$B(m,n)=\int_0^1 x^{m-1}(1-x)^{n-1}dx$$

Relation between Beta and Gamma functions:

$$B(m,n)=\frac{\Gamma(m)\Gamma(n)}{\Gamma(m+n)}$$

Properties

  • \(B(m,n)=B(n,m)\)
  • Beta function is symmetric
  • Used in probability distributions

Solved Examples

Example 1

Evaluate \( \Gamma(5) \)

$$\Gamma(5)=4!=24$$

Example 2

Evaluate \( B(2,3) \)

$$B(2,3)=\frac{\Gamma(2)\Gamma(3)}{\Gamma(5)}$$
$$=\frac{1! \times 2!}{4!}=\frac{2}{24}=\frac{1}{12}$$
